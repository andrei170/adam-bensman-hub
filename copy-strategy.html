Foundation

Copy Strategy - Adam Bensman (@roofstrategist)

Built: 2026-05-16 Brand voice: Operator-grounded roofing-sales register. Direct, sharp, results-language. The vocabulary of "close," "pitch," "doors," "canvassing," "sales floor," "lead," "claim," "storm," "retail," "rep," "owner," "team," "system," "playbook," "objection," "ramp," "book," "ticket." Numbers up front. No life-coach vagueness. The cadence of the 10-Second-Pitch YouTube video plus the Roofing Sales Survival Guide.

Voice rules

Speak in

  • Specific roles, specific revenue bands, specific team sizes, specific closing ratios
  • The vocabulary of the roofing-sales trade - "D2D," "storm-damage," "claims," "supplements," "insurance adjuster," "AOB," "AOR," "homeowner," "canvassing radius," "neighbourhood," "supplement margin," "production schedule," "ramp time" - never softened or translated
  • Decision-tree language ("if your rep ramp is sub-90-day on doors, the bottleneck is pitch system; if your reps ramp clean but churn at month-three, the bottleneck is retention; if your closers are at quota but your branch P&L is flat, the bottleneck is ops or marketing")
  • First-person operator voice ("I started in D2D in 2011," "I built into the COO role at a multi-state restoration company," "the Sales System runs in all 50 states, Canada, Australia, and Sweden")
  • The 13-years-in-the-game + former-COO + Owens-Corning-partner + RSRA-founder framing as observable fact, not as flex
  • The post-TAMKO acquisition (May 13) as observable fact, framed as the wedge timing, not as a TAMKO marketing claim

Do not speak in

  • Hedge-fund-analyst register or academic-finance vocabulary
  • Life-coach vagueness or self-development-influencer generality
  • Generic agency-coach language as a hero headline
  • Em dashes (commas, periods, or "-" only)
  • Triple-adjective lists or "not just X, but Y" structures
  • Income guarantees or roofing-license outcome promises - the cohort teaches sales operations and team-build operations, it does not promise roofing license outcomes or contractor certification
  • Anything that reads as competing with the Roofing STRONG Alliance - the cohort feeds it
  • Marketing-influencer language ("hack," "secret," "trick," "tap into," "unlock," "leverage") that conflicts with the alliance's values framework

Brand pillars

The four content pillars that underwrite every piece of copy across the funnel:

  1. The four modules are the spine. D2D and storm-damage closing systems, recruiting and retention, branch P&L + ops, personal-brand and content compounding. Every piece of content maps to one of the four. Every cohort grad walks all four.
  2. The cohort is the install version of the Sales System courses. Free content seeds the audience. Paid courses sell the playbook. The cohort walks the live operation with Adam on the call.
  3. 13 years, Owens Corning partner, RSRA-now-Roofing-STRONG-Alliance founder, former Roofing Company COO, Sales System in 50+ states plus Canada plus Australia plus Sweden, Amazon book. Stated as observable fact, never as flex. Authority comes from the operating track record, not from the headline.
  4. The cohort feeds the Roofing STRONG Alliance application; it does not compete with it. Grads who qualify route to the alliance as the natural high-touch back-end (TAMKO-owned). Grads who fit the $5MM-$13MM band route to 1-1 consulting. The cohort qualifies; it does not replace.

Avatars

Avatar A - "The Elite Roofing Sales Rep Ready to Build a Team" (40% of cohort applicants)

Age 25-35. Currently a D2D or storm-damage rep at a small-to-mid roofing company. Personal commission $200K-$500K. No formal team yet, or 1-2 direct reports building under them. Lives in a tier-1 or tier-2 US storm-state metro. Follows Adam on YouTube, listens to the Roof Strategist Podcast on the truck commute. Owns or has owned the Marketing Battle Pack. Wants to step into a team-build leadership role but has not been handed a team. Reads the cohort as "the way I learn branch-leader operations before I have a branch."

Lead-magnet hook: "Score your roofing operator readiness in 90 seconds. Find out whether the bottleneck is closing systems, team build, branch P and L, or marketing."

Cohort outcome promise: "By the end of week 8, the leadership operating system is mapped, the first SOPs are written, and you know what your next 90 days of building a team looks like."

Avatar B - "The Sub-$5M Roofing Company Owner-Operator" (35% of cohort applicants)

Age 30-45. Owns a roofing company at $1MM-$5MM annual revenue. Team of 5-15 producers (mix of canvassers, closers, project managers, supplementers). Knows the operation but the ceiling has felt real for the last 6-12 months. Owns or has owned Build Your Dream Team. Reads the cohort as "the upgrade from random podcast content and ad-hoc consulting calls."

Lead-magnet hook: "If your roofing company is stuck at sub-$5M, the bottleneck is almost always recruiting cadence or branch ops. Score it."

Cohort outcome promise: "By the end of week 8, the recruiting SOP is rewritten, the branch P and L map is redrawn, and the cohort has walked your operation as the live case study."

Avatar C - "The Storm Specialist Looking for the Retail Crossover" (15% of cohort applicants)

Age 28-45. Storm chaser or storm-damage specialist. Strong on insurance work, weaker on retail. Revenue is volatile by season + geography. Wants a retail playbook to flatten the revenue curve. Reads the cohort as "the four-module operating system that translates the storm playbook into retail."

Lead-magnet hook: "Score your readiness to add a retail revenue lane to your storm operation. Find out which of the four modules to build before you launch the retail funnel."

Cohort outcome promise: "By the end of week 8, you have the four playbooks (closing systems, team build, branch P and L, marketing), the SOPs, and a 90-day post-cohort plan for the retail launch."

Avatar D - "The Established $5MM-$13MM Operator Looking for the Next Layer" (10% of cohort applicants)

Higher-end of the segment. Already running a $5MM-$13MM roofing or restoration company. Reads the cohort for the peer network and the live-deal walks more than for the foundational content. Tends to upgrade to the standard-plus-operating-review tier ($8K). Often becomes the cohort case study and gets routed to 1-1 consulting post-cohort.
