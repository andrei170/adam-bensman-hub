Integration points + risk mitigation

Integration points

  1. Cohort application -> Stripe -> email tag. Typeform submission triggers a Stripe payment link in the confirmation, Stripe charge succeeds triggers the cohort:enrolled tag, the tag triggers the post-application sequence + the cohort kickoff prep flow.
  2. Scorecard -> email tag -> nurture routing. Roofing Operator Score completion triggers the appropriate scorecard tag (e.g. scorecard:owner:ops), the tag triggers the 14-day welcome flow with the routing built for that segment.
  3. Course purchase -> cohort cross-sell. Existing course storefront purchases trigger a soft cohort pre-sell drop in the post-purchase sequence (timed at Day 30 post-purchase for Marketing Battle Pack buyers; Day 14 post-purchase for Build Your Dream Team buyers).
  4. Cohort wrap -> alliance handoff. Cohort wrap call completion triggers the cohort:grad tag, the tag triggers the post-cohort routing flow with the alliance handoff at Day 7 (for cohort grads who qualify) and the 1-1 consulting routing at Day 14 (for cohort grads at the $5MM-$13MM band).

Risk mitigation

Risk Mitigation
Cohort applications under-fill in cycle 1 The 25-seat cap is the cohort floor. AG1 ships the cohort pre-sell sequence + the boosted organic + the podcast end-roll mechanic all together so cycle 1 has full audience attention. Worst case: cycle 1 runs at 15-20 seats, still hits $45K-$160K direct on the cycle 1 line.
TAMKO friction on the alliance-side cross-sell The alliance-side cross-sell is the optional Phase 4 layer. The cohort + scorecard + storefront cross-sell stand on their own. If TAMKO coordination is slow, the storefront-side cross-sell still drives $50K-$150K of the projected $80K-$250K cross-sell lift on its own.
Brand-handoff confusion (Roof Strategist vs The STRONG Roofer vs Roofing STRONG Alliance) Every cohort surface uses the cohort-specific brand naming ("The STRONG Operator Cohort"). The handoff between the renamed YouTube channel, the legacy course storefront, and the new alliance is structured into the bio-link router updates on Day 5. AG1 ships a one-page brand-handoff explainer for the cohort grad community channel on Day 7.
Income or roofing-license outcome claims Every cohort outcome promise includes the "results vary, depends on operator inputs, the cohort is not a roofing license or contractor certification" qualifier. The trades footer ships on the cohort landing + scorecard landing + every email.
Scorecard quiz fatigue (audience already trained to the FREE Roof Claims Crash Course) The Roofing Operator Score is positioned as a self-diagnosis tool, not as a free training. The 12-question format takes 90 seconds (vs the multi-video crash course commitment). The two assets coexist; the crash course captures cold traffic to top-of-funnel, the scorecard captures warm traffic to segmentation.
Email platform migration risk AG1 audits the existing email platform on Day 4. No migration unless the current platform cannot support the tagging matrix. If migration is required, it adds 5-7 days to the install timeline and ships during Phase 3.
Cohort time-cost ceiling on Adam The cohort is 4-6 hours per week DURING 8-week cycles, then dormant between cycles. 4 cycles per year = ~160 hours per year total. Lower than the existing podcast + YouTube + blog cadence. Adam's calendar is the constraint, not the cohort structure.

Decision points (in install order)

  1. Day 1 - Brand-colour lock. Adam chooses: legacy Roof Strategist palette / Roofing STRONG Alliance red+white / cohort-specific palette. AG1 ships AG1 gold as the default until Adam decides.
  2. Day 1 - Community platform. Skool / Circle / Slack. Skool is the default unless Adam already runs another.
  3. Day 2 - Cohort tier pricing. Standard tier ($3K) and standard-plus-operating-review tier ($8K). Adam can move both bands by ±$500 without re-architecting the funnel.
  4. Day 3 - Cohort cycle dates. 4 cycles for 2026, locked to Adam's calendar including alliance / TAMKO commitments.
  5. Day 4 - Scorecard platform. ScoreApp vs Typeform. AG1 recommendation Day 4 based on the feature-parity audit.
  6. Day 5 - Bio-link router destinations. Scorecard first, cohort landing second, course catalogue third on every handle (default), or adjusted if Adam prefers a different priority.
  7. Day 7 - Cross-sell architecture scope. Storefront-side cross-sell is in scope for Phase 4 (under Adam's full control). Alliance-side cross-sell is the optional Phase 4 layer that needs TAMKO-team coordination.
