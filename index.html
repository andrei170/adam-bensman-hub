01

The mid-tier mentorship cohort layer between the courses and the alliance is missing

The Marketing Battle Pack, Complete Sales Strategy, and Build Your Dream Team are the course versions of the promise. The cohort is the install version. Eight weeks, 25-seat cap, application-only, weekly live group call with you, biweekly office hours, private community channel for the duration. Four modules walked in order: D2D and storm-damage closing systems (the Sales System operationalised at cohort depth), recruiting and retention for the sales team (the Build Your Dream Team material walked live), branch P&L and ops for the $1MM-$13MM operator, personal-brand and content compounding for the sales-leader.

Working name: The STRONG Operator Cohort (leans on the existing brand naming post-rename - The STRONG Roofer channel and the Roofing STRONG Alliance share the same naming convention). Mid-tier mentorship pricing, two tiers. 4 cycles per year. Cohort grads who qualify route into the Roofing STRONG Alliance application as the natural high-touch back-end. The cohort feeds the alliance; it does not compete with it.

Cost of waiting

4 cohort cycles per year x 25 seats x mid-tier mentorship range = $300K-$800K direct Year-1 not yet collected. Each quarter the cohort does not exist is one cycle missed and one group of Build Your Dream Team buyers who graduated without a paid upgrade tier to enrol into.

Six months from now: $150K-$400K in cohort revenue never collected. The Build Your Dream Team buyers continue to exit the funnel with no productised upgrade above the course tier.

02

No segmenting quiz lead magnet on the front end

The FREE Roof Claims Crash Course captures email at signup but does not segment the audience by buyer type (rep / elite-rep / owner / storm-specialist), revenue band ($0-$500K through $13M+), or primary bottleneck (closing / recruiting / ops / marketing). Every subscriber gets the same nurture sequence regardless of whether they are a new rep buying the Marketing Battle Pack or a $4M operator ready to enrol in the cohort.

A Roofing Operator Score 12-question scorecard solves this in one move. Viewer enters current role, monthly revenue, team size, closing ratio, recruiting cadence, retention rate, primary lead source, marketing spend, primary bottleneck, training stack, average job value, and storm vs retail mix. Returns a 0-100 score, a 3-line bottleneck readout, and a segmented upgrade-path route. Email captures at the result. Drops into the podcast end-roll, the @roofstrategist IG bio-link router, the TikTok caption stack alongside the existing SMS demo mechanic, the YouTube descriptions, and the LinkedIn header.

Cost of waiting

5-8% audience-to-quiz floor on a multi-tens-of-thousands warm audience across YouTube, podcast, IG, and TikTok = 3K-6K scorecard completions in 90 days. 60-75% convert to email at the result = 2K-4.5K incremental email subscribers from existing audience in 90 days. $60K-$180K Year-1 nurture-driven lift, compounds for life of the list.

Six months from now: $30K-$90K never captured. The email list is six months smaller than it should be when the cohort opens for its first cycle.
"12 questions. 90 seconds. One number, three bottlenecks, one route out."
Compound pickup - window is 90 days
03

The post-acquisition cross-sell between the course storefront and the alliance is unbuilt

On May 13, TAMKO acquired RSRA's assets and relaunched as the Roofing STRONG Alliance by TAMKO with you continuing to lead. The alliance lives at strongbytamko.com. The course storefront lives at theroofstrategist.com. Today the two surfaces operate as separate brand entities. A Build Your Dream Team buyer running a $3M roofing company is a Roofing STRONG Alliance application candidate, but there is no clean routing path from the course product to the alliance application. A Roofing STRONG Alliance member joining for the TAMKO Edge contractor program is a cohort candidate and a Complete Sales Strategy buyer, but there is no clean routing path from the alliance back to the owned course catalogue.

The post-acquisition window is the right moment because attention is on the brand handoff and the audience is paying attention. In 12-18 months the integration will be settled and the cross-sell will feel retrofit rather than aligned. The work is brand-architecture, not net-new content production.

Cost of waiting

Cross-sell economics in a multi-brand operation of this scale typically add 5-15% incremental revenue from clean tier-clarity post-acquisition. Applied to the combined course catalogue, cohort, and alliance-application volume, the cross-sell variant adds $80K-$250K Year-1.

Six months from now: Another six months of Build Your Dream Team buyers exiting without an alliance handoff, and alliance members exiting without a cohort or course-catalogue handoff. The post-acquisition window is not permanent.
