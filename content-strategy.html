Content Strategy - Adam Bensman (@roofstrategist)

Built: 2026-05-16 Brand voice: Operator-grounded roofing-sales register. Direct, sharp, results-language. Numbers up front. Match the cadence of the 10-Second-Pitch YouTube video plus the Roofing Sales Survival Guide plus the Roof Strategist Podcast.

The job content has to do

The cohort lives between the free content (YouTube The STRONG Roofer + Roof Strategist Podcast + IG @roofstrategist + TikTok + blog) and the Roofing STRONG Alliance application (TAMKO-owned). Content has to do three things in 2026, in this order:

  1. Feed the scorecard. Every reel, short, and podcast episode pushes a soft CTA to the Roofing Operator Score quiz, the lead-magnet entry to the email list.
  2. Pre-sell the cohort. Specific module-depth reels and long-form podcast episodes (closing systems, recruiting and retention, branch P&L + ops, personal-brand and content compounding) seed the four cohort modules weeks before each cohort opens.
  3. Route warm viewers to the right lane. Cohort applicants self-serve into the cohort. Course-tier buyers route to Marketing Battle Pack / Complete Sales Strategy / Build Your Dream Team. Alliance-curious operators route to strongbytamko.com (TAMKO-owned). $5MM-$13MM operators route to 1-1 consulting.

The cohort is the new revenue line. The content does the marketing job for it. The course catalogue stays as the front-end. The alliance stays as the TAMKO-owned high-touch back-end.

Lane 3 - "Operator Reads" (cohort case studies + alliance handoff)

Once the inaugural cohort is in market, this lane carries the receipts. Until then, the credibility wall is the 13-year operator record, the Owens Corning partnership, the Roofing STRONG Alliance founder credential, the Sales System geographic reach (50 states + Canada + Australia + Sweden), the Amazon book, the multi-tens-of-thousands YouTube channel, and the weekly podcast cadence across every major platform.

Format: Long-form podcast episodes (40-60 min) plus written blog posts on blog.theroofstrategist.com. The format is a structured operator interview with a named cohort grad walking their specific operation: what was stuck, what got walked, what the cohort changed, the 90-day plan that came out of it.

Cadence: 1 per month, starting cohort cycle 2 (cohort grads from cycle 1 are the first wave of named voices on the wall).

Until cohort 1 wraps: The "Operator Reads" slot runs adjacent material - blog posts and podcast episodes covering the post-acquisition transition, the brand handoff to The STRONG Roofer + Roofing STRONG Alliance, the four-module operating system at depth, and the working-relationship case studies from the 1-1 consulting practice (named operators where they have given permission, anonymised where they have not).

Photoshoot brief

Plan for a half-day photo + b-roll shoot in the first 30 days of install. Outputs feed cohort landing page hero, scorecard landing page, all email assets, all content thumbnails, paid ad creative, and the post-acquisition rebrand pieces.

Locations: Roofing job site (single-family residential, in-progress install), at-desk reviewing a branch P&L sheet (no rep names visible), in-truck driving (b-roll for the D2D vocabulary moments), and a clean studio backdrop for the talking-head segments.

Shot list:

  • 1 hero shot for the cohort landing page (full-body or 3/4 shot, neutral background, sharp lighting)
  • 2 secondary hero options (one job-site, one at-desk)
  • 6-8 b-roll cutaways for VSL production (hand on a recruiting board, hand on a branch P&L sheet, walking on a roof, talking with a homeowner on a door, gesturing at a whiteboard with the four-module map)
  • 4 thumbnail-ready vertical shots for short-form content
  • 1 founder photo for the About Adam section on the cohort landing
  • 1 alliance-handoff shot if appropriate (TAMKO branding visible, optional, with TAMKO coordination)

Wardrobe: Brand-neutral but trade-aligned (work pants, brand-clean polo or t-shirt, work boots for the job-site shots; clean polo + jeans for the at-desk and studio shots). No suit, no influencer-aesthetic loungewear.

Style: Natural light where possible. No heavy retouching. Match the register of the YouTube channel: direct, operator-grounded, no influencer polish.

Risk mitigation

  • Post-acquisition register risk. The Roofing STRONG Alliance is now TAMKO-owned. The cohort content must not adopt sales-hustle language that conflicts with the alliance's "raising the standard" values framework. Every Lane 1 / Lane 2 / Lane 3 piece gets a values-framework pass before publish.
  • Geographic regulation risk. Roofing is licensed at the state level and licensing rules vary. The cohort teaches sales operations and team-build operations, not roofing licensure. Every cohort outcome promise includes a "results vary, depends on operator inputs, the cohort is not a roofing license or contractor certification" qualifier.
  • Brand-handoff register risk. The YouTube channel renamed from @TheRoofStrategist to @TheSTRONGRoofer. The IG and TikTok handles stay at @roofstrategist. The course storefront brand stays as The Roof Strategist. The alliance is the Roofing STRONG Alliance by TAMKO. Get the naming right on every content surface or the audience will notice.
